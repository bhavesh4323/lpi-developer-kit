## What I Learned
1. **SMILE isn’t about data volume – it’s about decision framing**
   - Case study examples and sports have shown us this: you don’t need sensors everywhere. You need sensors and ontology that target important decisions.

2. **Ontology is the most transferable asset**
   - In the automotive case study, the ontology of failure modes outlasts the predictive model. In sports, capturing the coach’s tacit knowledge of injury patterns, overtraining signs, and tactical load becomes far more resilient than any dashboard.

3. **Context is superior to bare metrics**
   - A high heart rate is neither good nor bad. A high load is neither healthy nor harmful. The twin’s worth lies in its ability to capture context, including recent travel, schedule, player experience, sleep, and personal life stressors. This holds true equally in healthcare, manufacturing, and sports.

4. **Phases are sequential but not fixed**
   - The healthcare case study example (cs-004) explicitly chose to end at Phase 4 (AI-generated clinical governance was not yet set up). This is practical and wise. In sports, a club may decide to end at Phase 4 (dashboard-driven decision support) and forego Phases 5-6 (AI-driven autonomous recommendations).
